About Maggie

We put this website about her together to help her get back home safe and happy!

Maggie Leggett is sweet, microchipped, fixed, and fully vaccinated. She’s super soft and gentle, and loves babies, children, college girls, middle aged women and elderly people. She particularly loves attention, pets and her favorite treats.

She's easily frightened, but she's not aggressive, she'll just try to run and hide. Her recall is quite bad. She knows "come here" but rarely does, but she is good with "sit!", "stay!", "no!", "drop it!", and she likes to be told that she's a good girl.

She can also be a little sassy (she will huff at you - you have been warned!), and she's a little spoiled, and sometimes she'll refuse to eat unless she's fed on the sofa.

She also likes a good blanket, soft toys with squeakers, quiet spaces, hiding under beds, and snuggling on sofas.

Most importantly she has lots of friends and family who love her very, very much!

Maggie’s health

Maggie has some bad skin allergies, anxiety, and motion sickness, so she needs a little extra care. But don’t worry, she’s very easy to look after, and we'll drop everything to come pick her up as soon as we hear from you!

Microchip location

Maggie's microchip should be between her shoulder blades.

Maggie’s favourite foods

Maggie's usual dinner is half a can of Purina One (chicken or beef) in the evening. She loves it. Often a little too much to bother chewing it...
Her favourite foods are turkey, chicken, fish, and eggs. She'll do anything for poultry. If you have to coax her out from anywhere, heat up some turkey or some shredded white meat from a rotisserie chicken, and let her see you dangle it for her. A chicken and cheese omelette is also a tempting meal if she's too scared to eat. She also loves her Greenies dental chews!
If you have to buy her anything, we'll happily pay you back!

Maggie's medications

Maggie is currently taking Apoquel for her allergies. Unfortunately her skin allergies are quite bad, and if she even misses it for one day she starts itching the hair off her fluffy tummy.
She eats her Apoquel in her food every evening. She's good at finding pills that are hidden in things though, so some peanut butter might be necessary! Obviously just make sure Xylitol isn't on the ingredients list.
She takes 5.4mg tablets and we get hers from Sam's Club because they're half the price there!

Maggie also takes Simparica Trio to protect against fleas/ticks/heartworms and she takes it on the 18th of every month.
Please contact us or take her to a vet instead of giving her anything for heartworms! Heartworm preventatives are for larvae, not grown worms, and can be lethal for dogs with heartworm infections!
